How CareerQuest works

Students pick a career, then complete a board of quests that turn vague curiosity into a confident, evidence-based decision.

🔍 Recon

Meet a mentor and walk through their real day, hour by hour. Map every route in — including the ones without four years of debt. Run the numbers in the ROI calculator.

🤝 Field Work

Book a real informational interview (we hand you the script), complete a free Forage job simulation built by actual companies, and shadow a professional for a day.

⚖️ Reality Check

Answer the 40-year question, pass the cost-vs-salary quiz, and deliver a verdict: pursue, maybe, or pass — with reasons they can defend to a parent.

Questions parents ask

Isn't this just another career quiz?

No. Quizzes guess at personality. CareerQuest has students do the actual work of a job through simulations, talk to real professionals, and confront the real costs — the same way a thoughtful adult would research a major purchase.

My teen has no idea what they want. Will this help?

That's exactly who it's for. Exploring three or four worlds — and ruling some out — is a win. Discovering you'd hate a job before paying for the degree is just as valuable as discovering you'd love it.

Are the salary numbers reliable?

They're national medians from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, and every career links straight to the BLS source so you can verify current local figures. They're starting points for conversation, not guarantees.

Is this anti-college?

Not at all. Research shows a bachelor's degree pays off handsomely for most people. The point is to choose a path on purpose — degree, trade, or apprenticeship — instead of defaulting into debt and discovering the fit later.

How long does it take?

The core game runs about 2–4 hours per career. The full guided curriculum is designed for a semester elective or a self-paced summer project.
