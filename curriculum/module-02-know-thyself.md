# Module 2 — Know Thyself

> **Time:** 45–60 min · **Worksheet:** Self-inventory (below)

## The big idea

You can't match yourself to a career if you don't know yourself first. This isn't a personality quiz that spits out "you should be an architect." It's a quick, honest inventory of what energizes you, what drains you, and what you're willing to trade.

## Why it matters

Two people can have the same job and one thrives while the other burns out — because the *fit* is different. Pay attention to the texture of work you enjoy, not just the title.

## Four lenses

Work through each in your notebook:

**1. Energy.** What activities make you lose track of time? When do you feel most "in the zone"? When do you feel drained?

**2. Strengths.** What do people come to you for? What's easy for you that others find hard?

**3. Values & trade-offs.** Rank these by what matters most to you right now: high pay · job security · helping people · creativity · flexibility/freedom · prestige · low stress · variety · working with your hands · working with ideas.

**4. Constraints & willingness.** Honestly: How much school are you willing to do? How much debt is acceptable? Do you want to work indoors or outdoors, with people or solo, on a screen or with tools?

## Activity

Write one sentence that captures your current self-portrait, like:

> "I get energy from solving puzzles and building things, I'm strong at math and explaining ideas, I value flexibility over prestige, and I'd rather not take on more than ~$30k of debt."

This sentence becomes your filter. As you explore worlds in later modules, you'll hold each one up against it.

## Self-inventory worksheet

1. Three activities that make you lose track of time.
2. Two things people regularly ask your help with.
3. Your top three values from the list above.
4. Your honest limits: max years of school, max debt, indoor/outdoor, people/solo.
5. Your one-sentence self-portrait.

## Deliverable

Your one-sentence self-portrait plus the inventory. Keep it visible — you'll use it every time you scout a new career.
